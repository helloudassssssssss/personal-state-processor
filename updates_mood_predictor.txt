Model Performance Evaluation
After training the Linear Regression model, I evaluated its accuracy using three key metrics: MAE, MSE, and RÂ². Hereâ€™s what they mean:

- Mean Absolute Error (MAE): 0.44
This means that, on average, the model's predictions are 0.44 points away from the actual mood scores.

Lower MAE is better since it indicates smaller prediction errors.
In this case, 0.44 is a low error, meaning the model is making fairly accurate predictions.
- Mean Squared Error (MSE): 0.26
MSE measures the average squared difference between predicted and actual mood scores.

Since the errors are squared, larger errors impact the score more.
A lower value like 0.26 suggests that the model rarely makes large mistakes.
However, since the values are squared, MSE is less interpretable than MAE, so we use it mainly for optimization.
- RÂ² Score (Coefficient of Determination): 0.75
RÂ² tells us how well the model explains the variance in mood scores.

0.75 means that 75% of the changes in mood can be explained by sleep, stress, and exercise.
The closer RÂ² is to 1.0, the better the model fits the data.
A score of 0.75 is considered good, meaning the model captures most of the patterns in the dataset.
